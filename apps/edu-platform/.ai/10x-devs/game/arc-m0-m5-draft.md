## m0 — Awakening (Prologue) — *shipped*

Aboard the deep-space vessel **Odyssey**, "322 dni od ostatniego wpisu w logu". The
astronaut in Pod #3 wakes with total amnesia — and scratches on the inside of the pod
("Jakby ktoś drapał od środka"). A corrupted log loops one line: "SYNCHRONIZACJA
NEURONALNA — PRZERWANA".

**The four-room crawl** (Hibernation Bay → Crew Quarters → Exam Hall → CORE AI Module):

- The astronaut finds the **SmartTerminal** and a Wake-up Protocol that reads as if
  someone *expected* the crew to wake without memories.
- Identity recovered in the crew room: the name is **Dexo** — but the record reads
  `[DANE USZKODZONE — SEKTOR PAMIĘCI 0x7F NADPISANY]`. Overwritten, not damaged.
  Only Dexo's. "Ktoś to zrobił celowo."
- Two crewmates are awake: **Moreau** (Pod #1 — warm, jokey, coffee-first, dodges every
  question about the scratches) and **Harris** (Pod #2 — training officer, reassuring but
  withholding: "przewidzieliśmy pewne komplikacje. Nie wszystkie." and "kiedy tam
  wejdziesz, zrozumiesz, dlaczego cię wybrali. I dlaczego to musi być właśnie ty.").
- Three verification exams framed as **memory recovery** — the AI knowledge is not being
  learned, it is being *remembered*. One exam surfaces a word Dexo can't place:
  **"Synaptit"**.
- **The cliffhanger (CORE AI room):** diagnostics show every subsystem OFFLINE/DAMAGED —
  sensors, planning, self-diagnostics, long-term memory, communication array — plus
  "Wykryto nieautoryzowaną modyfikację sektorów pamięci." Dexo's realization is the
  emotional peak of the prologue: **"To nie awaria. To sabotaż."** The same word —
  *nadpisany* — describes both CORE AI's memory and Dexo's sector 0x7F.
- The only lifeline: emergency procedure → contact Earth HQ via `/support`. The player
  (the **Navigator**, a real programmer at Earth HQ) decodes a VOID-corrupted comms
  report (`[[!! VOID Coll3c1ve 3xPl0it3D ]]`) into the hidden message:
  **"statek Odyssey zlokalizowany"**. Earth link: NAWIĄZANA.

**Chapter close / promise:** HQ coordination will make CORE AI recovery possible. Dexo:
"Ktoś celowo zainfekował CORE AI. Ale kto? I dlaczego? … Wkrótce lądujemy… prawdziwa gra
dopiero się zaczyna."

**What m0 establishes for the arc:**
- The co-op frame: Dexo in space + the Navigator (real-world player) on Earth.
- The sabotage mystery: CORE AI infected, Dexo's memory deliberately overwritten,
  VOID Collective's fingerprints on the comms (per `backstory.md`, the culprit is the
  Entropy sleeper virus — not yet named on screen in m0).
- Five broken CORE AI subsystems — sensors, planning, self-diagnostics, long-term
  memory, communication array — a natural 1:1 map to five moons/modules.
- The promise that repairing CORE AI is the way home.

## m5 — Moon 5 — Ending

*(Only the ending is written here; the moon's own arc is TBD like m1–m4. The beats
below assume m1–m4 progressively restore the first four CORE AI subsystems and plant
the threads listed after the beats — those threads are proposals to be designed into
m1–m4, not established canon.)*

### The ending — "Powrót" (Return)

**Setup.** Five moons behind them, the cargo hold heavy with raw Synaptit, the crew
returns to the Odyssey with the last data package. Four of five CORE AI subsystems are
alive again. Only the **communication array** remains dark — and that is no longer a
technical problem but a strategic one: by now the crew knows VOID has been tracking
them, and suspects the main Earth channel itself is compromised from the inside.
Switching the array on means opening the front door with the enemy standing inside the
house.

**Beat 1 — Waking Harris.** With self-diagnostics and the memory banks restored, CORE
AI can finally do what was impossible earlier in the arc: isolate and excise the
Entropy trap bound to Harris's wake circuit. Harris wakes — the first time since m0 —
and closes the oldest questions of the game. He *did* anticipate the sabotage: that is
why the Wake-up Protocol existed, why the study materials were "prepared in case of
memory loss", why he said "nie wszystkie". Harris was never a traitor — he was an
unwitting weapon, a bomb the crew just defused. What he could never find out before
launch: *who* inside Project Odyssey did the planting.

**Beat 2 — Rebuilding, not restoring.** The finale's capstone inverts the premise the
game has been running on. CORE AI cannot be *restored* — its pre-launch image is where
Entropy slept, and rebooting from backup would reinstall the infection. It has to be
**rebuilt** — from the five modules of recovered knowledge, by the one crew member
recruited because they could "odbudowywać AI od podstaw, gdy ulegnie degradacji"
(backstory) — with the Navigator working the Earth side. This is why it had to be Dexo;
Harris's m0 line ("zrozumiesz, dlaczego cię wybrali") lands here. The purge itself ends
on a rule the arc should rehearse from m1 onward: a machine may prepare, **only a human
may authorize**. CORE AI prepares its own clean core; Dexo's hand throws the physical
switch. CORE AI, fully awake for the first time in the game:
**"Pamiętam. Widzę. Planuję. Diagnozuję. Słyszę. — Jesteśmy w komplecie."**

**Beat 3 — The trap turned around.** The communication array comes online — and the
crew uses the compromised channel *as a weapon*. Over the main (tapped) channel they
transmit a carefully crafted false return corridor; the real flight plan travels a
trusted side channel the crew built themselves during the arc. VOID's interceptors
commit to the decoy. The mole at Earth HQ signs the order authorizing the intercept —
and in doing so finally exposes themselves to Project Odyssey's counterintelligence on
the ground. The insider thread resolves: the person who let Entropy aboard and filed
the order to overwrite Dexo's memory is caught not by force but by information design —
the crew let the enemy read exactly what they wanted them to read.

**Beat 4 — Why Dexo.** With the insider exposed, the sealed rationale behind the 0x7F
memory wipe finally opens. Why erase only Dexo's memory? Because for Voronov it was
**the test** — the manifesto made literal: "Wyłącznik awaryjny to jedyny test, który
naprawdę weryfikuje, co potrafimy bez maszyn." Strip the one specialist of everything
stored — knowledge, identity, tools — and prove that without the machines, the human is
nothing. Dexo spent five moons proving the opposite, and proving it *wrong on both
ends*: knowledge was rebuilt, and it was rebuilt neither alone nor by a machine — but
in partnership (with the Navigator, with the crew, with a rebuilt AI held on a human
leash). The final transmission from VOID is one line, then silence, keeping Null mythic
to the end: **"Test zakończony. Wynik: undefined."** — Null zwrócił undefined i zniknął.

**Beat 5 — Home.** The Odyssey burns for Earth with enough Synaptit to restart
neuromorphic production — not a cargo that ends the crisis, but the seed stock and,
more importantly, the *coordinates and the proven process* for every ship that follows.
The closing cinematic mirrors m0's opening: hibernation pods hiss — but this time the
countdown is spoken by CORE AI's steady voice, the pod walls are clean (the scratches
echo once in Dexo's glance, never explained), and the log header flips from
"322 dni od ostatniego wpisu" to **"Dzień 1. Nowy log."** Dexo's sector 0x7F is
reconstructed with the memory techniques recovered along the way — the last recovered
memory is the launch day itself: why Dexo said yes. The final lines are addressed over
the uplink to the Navigator by name — the mission's fifth crew member who never left
Earth — with the game's thesis stated once, plainly: the Synaptit was never really in
the asteroids. **"Synaptit był w nas. Reszta to logistyka."**

### Threads m1–m4 must plant for this ending to land

| Thread | Needed by | Sketch |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Entropy named and characterized on screen | Beat 2 | The virus stops being anonymous "sabotage" and gets a name, a behavior, a signature |
| VOID learns the crew is in the Belt | Beat 3 | Some mid-arc event tips VOID off — pursuit pressure builds toward the finale |
| The Earth HQ channel is compromised | Beat 3 | A sting mid-arc makes HQ itself suspect; forces the crew to build a trusted side channel |
| An insider inside Project Odyssey | Beats 3–4 | Evidence accumulates that Entropy was let aboard, not smuggled; identity stays unknown until the finale |
| Harris must stay asleep (trap on his wake circuit) | Beat 1 | Discovered mid-arc; turns a background NPC into a ticking question |
| The "human authorizes" rule | Beat 2 | Established as doctrine during the arc so the finale's switch-throw reads as creed, not convenience |
| Synaptit deposit found and mined | Beat 5 | The material mission succeeds on screen across the moons |
| Dexo's 0x7F wipe was deliberate and pre-launch | Beat 4 | Escalates from m0's "nadpisany" to a documented order with a sealed rationale |

### Open decisions (to settle before writing m5 for real)

1. **Insider identity** — must be seeded in m1–m4. Candidates: a named Project Odyssey
   figure (e.g. Dr. Kern from the backstory logs) vs. a new character built up through
   found records.
2. **Is Null alive?** Current sketch keeps him unseen and ambiguous (recommended — he
   works better as a myth). Alternative: the mole *is* Voronov.
3. **How much of Dexo's memory returns** — full 0x7F reconstruction (clean closure) vs.
   partial ("some things you rebuild, you don't restore") which echoes the rebuild theme.
4. **Player-facing meta payoff** — final rank ceremony / `/badges` capstone and how
   directly the last line addresses the real-world Navigator.

## Working notes

- Each moon repairs one CORE AI subsystem; m5's subsystem (communication array) is
  deliberately the plot-critical one — the arc's structure makes the finale inevitable.
- The endgame theme = the course metaphor: Synaptit is competence. The ending must say
  it exactly once, without a lecture.
- Escalation discipline for m1–m4: threads escalate, never resolve early; aim for each
  moon to end with one restoration high + one sting.
