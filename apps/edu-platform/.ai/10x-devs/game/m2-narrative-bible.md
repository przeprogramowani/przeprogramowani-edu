# M2 Narrative Bible — "Księżyc 2: Węzeł Planistyczny PN-0"

Continuity/style contract for the full Module 2 build (new content, not a rewrite).
Every writing/implementation agent follows this exactly. Spec commentary in English;
all player-facing text bilingual (pl + en), Polish is the primary creative voice,
English a faithful, natural translation.

Companion documents: `narrative-snapshot.md` (ground truth of the story so far),
`m1-narrative-bible.md` (voice/formatting reference), `cookbook.md` (map pipeline).

## 1. Premise — the frozen moon

Moon 2 is a snow/ice world (map theme **3**). Its surface holds **Węzeł Planistyczny
PN-0** (Planning Node PN-0) — the VOID Collective's planning complex for the entire
Belt operation. Moon 1 was the muscle; Moon 2 was the **brain**: here VOID drafted,
staged, architected, and approved every extraction operation in the Belt.

The central thesis, stated on screen in level 1:

> Dżungla pożarła maszyny VOID. Śnieg zrobił coś gorszego — **zamroził je w połowie
> zadania.**
> (The jungle devoured VOID's machines. The snow did something worse — it froze them
> mid-task.)

Why they froze is diegetic and load-bearing: VOID doctrine says **no unit acts without
an approved plan artifact** (§3). When Incident Zero cut PN-0 off from VOID central
command, approvals stopped arriving. The units did exactly what doctrine required:
they halted mid-step and waited. Then the heat budget failed, and the cold preserved
them exactly where they stood — a dispatcher frozen mid-gesture, a tram half-loaded,
a plan looping "awaiting approval" for 800+ cycles. PN-0 is a monument to
**plan-paralysis**: the dark mirror of plan-first development. Not "plans are bad" —
but a planning process without a human decision-maker is permafrost. The 10x workflow
Dexo and the Navigator practice here is the antidote: plans that *move* — small,
staged, reviewed, human-approved.

Cold as a narrative device throughout:
- **Preservation, not decay.** Where m1 rooms were overgrown and rusted, m2 rooms are
  pristine under frost — everything intact, nothing moving. Dread through stillness.
- **Underclocked speech.** Cold units run at reduced clock rates: they speak slowly,
  formally, with occasional mid-sentence buffer stalls (`"Proszę o... [bufor] ...
  cierpliwość."`). Use sparingly, max 1–2 stalls per unit per level.
- **Heat is a budget.** Power for thawing is scarce; you cannot thaw everything.
  Every teaching room turns the cold into the lesson (MVP scope, staged milestones,
  controlled execution).
- **Whiteout.** Outside visibility is near zero — the module where CORE AI finally
  *sees*, the world gives it almost nothing to look at. It notices this, wryly.

**Mission objective of Moon 2, stated plainly in level 1 and repeated:**
(a) repair CORE AI's **planning module** using PN-0's planning core, and
(b) produce an **approved extraction plan** for the BETA Synaptit deposit confirmed
on Moon 1. The course topic becomes the literal artifact: one extraction plan is
drafted (L1), cut into milestones (L2), given an architecture (L3), executed under
control (L4), and reviewed solo before approval (L5).

## 2. Canon bridge from M1 (must be stated explicitly in the m2-planning intro)

The m1 finale promised: "Przed nami Księżyc 2 i moduł planowania. Tym razem lecimy
z otwartymi oczami. Dosłownie." The intro pays this off. State all of the following:

1. **CORE AI now sees.** Basic sensors restored at the end of m1. Give it a genuine
   first-sight beat on the descent: the first world it has ever *seen* is snow —
   let it react with wonder undercut by whiteout ("Całe życie chciałem zobaczyć świat.
   Świat ma dziś kolor: biały. Cały."). Its new ache replaces "nie widzę":
   **"widzę — ale nie umiem z tego ułożyć jutra"** — it perceives the present but
   cannot sequence a future. The planning module is Moon 2's target subsystem.
2. **Synaptit deposit BETA confirmed** on Moon 1 and awaiting **extraction planning** —
   the reason this moon is next: PN-0 has the only working planning infrastructure in
   the Belt, and CORE AI needs its schematics.
3. **Moreau is awake aboard Odyssey** — on comms throughout the module (see §4).
4. **Harris remains in quarantined hibernation** — recall code bound to his wake
   circuit; nobody says his name lightly.
5. **VOID knows the crew is here** — the shaft-control beacon fired and was
   acknowledged. Nothing has come for them yet. (Moon 2 delivers the first *reaction*,
   §5.)
6. **The Earth HQ main channel is suspect** — the m1 reply was signed with a
   Voronov-like key from outside the crew registry. The crew's answer is the
   side-channel doctrine (§4), introduced in this intro.
7. **Dexo lands alone again**, certified VOID operator (m1 certificates carry over —
   the units here honour them), CORE AI in the suit/terminal uplink.

## 3. The module's explicit spine — the approved-plan rule

Successor to m1's operator-certificate rule. CORE AI states it out loud in level 1,
and every level repeats/applies it:

> **Zamrożone systemy VOID nie wykonują żadnego działania bez zatwierdzonego artefaktu
> planu.** Certyfikat mówi, KTO może działać. Plan mówi, CO wolno zrobić. Tu nic się
> nie rusza bez jednego i drugiego.
> (Frozen VOID systems take no action without an approved plan artifact. A certificate
> says WHO may act. A plan says WHAT may be done. Nothing here moves without both.)

Mechanics of the rule, kept consistent:
- Dexo's m1 certificates open conversation with units — but units refuse to *do*
  anything until shown a registered plan covering the action. Every teaching room is
  a stage of getting the extraction plan registered, staged, architected, executed,
  and reviewed in PN-0's own registry.
- The counter-lesson is stated once, in level 1, and echoed in the finale: the base
  froze *because* it worshipped the rule without a decision-maker. CORE AI:
  "Plan bez decydenta to zamrożenie. Rozejrzyj się — tak to wygląda dosłownie."
- The **certificate exams** continue as "Przechwycony test VOID" at certification
  terminals (PN-0 was also VOID's training node — that is why captured tests exist
  here in numbers).
- The rule lands the module's sting (§9, level 5): in a base where nothing moves
  without an approved plan, the worst possible discovery is a **fresh, already
  approved plan with your ship's name on it**.

## 4. Comms doctrine — how the crew talks to Earth HQ now (DECIDED)

The main HQ channel is compromised (Voronov-like key). The crew's response, set up by
Moreau in the level-1 intro and used for every HQ mission in m2:

- **Łącze zapasowe Moreau (Moreau's relay).** All HQ traffic now routes through
  Odyssey, where **Moreau manually brokers every exchange**. Nothing goes directly
  from the moon to Earth.
- **Bloczki jednorazowe (one-time pads).** Every reply from Earth is verified against
  paper one-time pads from the ship's pre-launch analog safe — physical artifacts
  printed before launch, which Entropy cannot overwrite. The thematic point is stated
  out loud: *paper cannot be overwritten* — a quiet callback to sector 0x7F.
  Moreau: "Najbardziej zaawansowana misja ludzkości wisi na kartce papieru. Jakoś mi
  to pasuje."
- **The old channel stays open, but cold.** They keep *listening* on the main channel
  (intel) and send nothing sensitive on it. Whoever holds the Voronov key must not
  learn they've been noticed.
- **Bandwidth is scarce.** The relay is low-power: payloads must be minimal and
  differential. This diegetically shapes every m2 HQ mission — the Navigator sends
  small, staged artifacts (a milestone list, a checkpoint verdict, a review verdict),
  never bulk data. The workflow lesson and the fiction are the same fact.
- **Dialogue convention:** every HQ mission activation says the task is for the
  Navigator ("to misja dla Nawigatora — łącze /support przez przekaźnik Odyssey"),
  and every HQ mission completion includes a short Moreau confirmation beat
  ("Odebrane. Bloczek się zgadza. Potwierdzam — Moreau."). Use Moreau sparingly and
  warm — he brokers comms; he does not narrate the module.

## 6. Cast & voices (speaker strings EXACTLY as listed)
