In previous lessons, you saw two of the most well-known environments for working with agents - IDE and terminal. In this lesson, we take a step further: we look at tools where the center of the interface is increasingly no longer a code file, but an agent session.

At first, it may look suspiciously familiar: you open a new environment, see a prompt field, a task list, agent statuses... and the code disappears from the foreground. Exactly - where did the editor go?

Sounds like a recipe for code mess? Only if you treat the agent as an autonomous employee without supervision. That's why safe use of such tools still relies on things you know well: a clean repository state, limited scope of changes, tests, reviews, diff control, careful access to secrets, and cost awareness.

During 10xDevs you will dive deeper into worktrees, parallel work, results evaluation, and choosing the right environment for the task. For now, treat this lesson as a hint about a new category of tools: why they exist, what they try to simplify, and where they demand more discipline from you than a classic editor.
